Rhetorical Analysis of Brat Songs

Ancient rhetorical theory can often get lost in translation when it comes to contemporary pop culture, but those theories are still very present in our everyday lives. This rhetorical analysis considers the feminist rhetoric within the remixes of two songs from CharliXCX's 2024 album Brat and it's completely different but also still brat, grounded in the theories of rhetorician Desiderius Erasmus. The analysis considers the conceptual, interactive, and technical aspects of a remix as rhetorical elements which develop each song's deeper meaning. Ultimately, the discussion claims that these remixes are evidence of the power of rhetoric to affect an international, cross-class, and cross-genre audience.

The original essay is intended for an in-depth academic discussion of the topic for a scholar of rhetoric. That discussion is translated into a brief ten-minute poster presentation for fellow graduate students outside the field of rhetoric studies. It is also translated into a twenty-minute conference presentation for the 2025 Creative Women's Writing Association conference, an audience composed of feminist writers and scholars across a variety of fields.

This project originated as an assignment for the course Theories of Invention in Writing.
