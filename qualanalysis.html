Qualitative Analysis Report

One of the main goals of any university degree program is producing employable graduates. This qualitative analysis surveys the available job market at the time for positions that I might seek to apply for (entry-level technical writing positions in technologically related fields), with the intent to discover (a) the work available, (b) skills required and expected, and (c) required and preferred qualifications. The document offers a literature review, detailed methods of data collection, a breakdown of results with visual data organizers, and a discussion of what the analysis means for a Master's student halfway through a degree in Technical Communication and Rhetoric. While this project is highly tailored toward my own personal career interests and academic experience, it serves to convey and explain complex information.

This report is directed toward the professor instructing the course, though it may also be helpful for a graduate advisor or fellow graduate students of the Technical Communication and Rhetoric program interested in the same niche of the field. The topic is self-selected for my own learning purposes.

This project originated as a self-proposed assignment for the course Foundations in Technical Communication.
